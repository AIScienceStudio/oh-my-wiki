# Ali Behrouz

> Lead author of the [[Nested Learning]] paper and Student Researcher at Google Research.

## Overview

Ali Behrouz is the primary architect of the [[Nested Learning]] paradigm and lead author of the foundational paper "Nested Learning: The Illusion of Deep Learning Architectures" published at NeurIPS 2025. He is listed as a Student Researcher at Google Research, working under the supervision of Vahab Mirrokni (VP and Google Fellow).

Behrouz is also associated with the [[Titans Architecture]], the predecessor to [[Hope Architecture|Hope]] that introduced the concept of a neural memory module learning to update itself at test time. The [[Nested Learning]] paper represents a significant theoretical advancement, providing the overarching framework that explains why Titans-style systems work and how to generalize them.

His co-authors on the NL paper include Meisam Razaviyayn (USC), Peilin Zhong (Google Research), and Vahab Mirrokni (Google Research). The team has indicated plans to release code and data on GitHub, though as of the ArXivIQ analysis, the code for the related Titans paper had not yet been published.

## Key Contributions

- **[[Nested Learning]]** -- the multi-level optimization paradigm (NeurIPS 2025)
- **[[Hope Architecture]]** -- self-modifying sequence model with continuum memory
- **[[Titans Architecture]]** -- predecessor architecture with neural long-term memory
- **[[Optimizers as Memory]]** -- insight that standard optimizers are associative memory modules
- **[[Continuum Memory System]]** -- generalization of binary memory into a frequency spectrum
